Quatro Story

Old Quatro

Quatro was a canned, fruit-flavoured carbonated drink produced from 1982 to 1989 in the United Kingdom. From 1983 to 1989 it was commonly available in the UK, though production and sale of the drink ceased there in the mid-1980s. Green in colour, its name derived from the four fruits used: pineapple, orange, passion fruit and grapefruit.

New Quatro

New Quatro was reborn in 2023, and while the previous four fruits were pineapple, orange, passion fruit, and grapefruit, the new Quatro uses apple, orange, lime, and pineapple. It's closer to the fruits and flavors that people are familiar with, but we want to showcase something new, something unforgettable, something unique to Quatro.

Logo

The Quatro logo features the brand name "Quatro" in bold, stylized letters, with the "Q" in the name being the most prominent character. The "Q" has a unique design where the tail of the letter extends above and then loops around to form the circle part of the "Q."

The logo's design aims to convey a sense of fun, energy, and vibrancy. The stylized "Q" and the bright color palette gave the Quatro brand a distinctive and memorable identity.

The new logo features multiple colors, including #ffdbb3, #8e272d, #557b3f, and #d9e021. These colors are used to fill various parts of the logo, representing four different fruits and tastes and creating a vibrant and eye-catching design.

There are different shapes and paths in the logo, such as a stylized letter "Q" with a leaf-like structure. It describes the process of creating a delicious beverage from raw materials extracted from fresh fruits. This signify the natural ingredients in the beverage or represent the brand's commitment to the environment.
